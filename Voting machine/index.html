CISA Security Advisories

  • June 2022: CISA issued advisory on 9 vulnerabilities in Dominion Voting Systems software (16 states)
  • 2023: University of Michigan security analysis found vulnerabilities in "practically every significant attack surface" of Dominion ImageCast X machines
  • Key vulnerability: Software flaw allowing malware to spread from election management system to every voting machine in jurisdiction
  • Authentication broken: Smartcard protocol "completely broken," enabling counterfeit technician cards
  • No evidence: CISA found no evidence these vulnerabilities had been exploited in actual elections

Key International Lessons

  • Paper Ballot Primacy: Most democracies emphasize paper ballots as the most transparent and auditable method
  • Public Verifiability: German Constitutional Court ruled citizens must be able to verify election processes "without special expert knowledge"
  • Security Concerns: Netherlands abandoned DREs after researchers demonstrated side-channel attacks via radio emissions
  • Estonia Success Factors: National e-identity infrastructure, vote changing allowed during advance voting, mandatory paper option
  • Canadian Redundancy: After 2018 technical failure affecting 51 municipalities, jurisdictions restored paper ballot redundancy
  • Australia Transparency: Uses OCR scanning with scrutineer oversight for ballot counting

Expert Recommendations for Modernization

1. Universal Paper Ballot Requirement

  • Replace all paperless voting machines with systems producing voter-verifiable paper ballots
  • Over 98% of jurisdictions already compliant as of 2024
  • Endorsed by 100+ leading computer scientists, NIST research, and Senate Select Committee on Intelligence

2. Risk-Limiting Audits (RLAs)

  • Implement "gold standard" post-election audits using statistical sampling
  • Provide high confidence that reported outcomes are correct
  • Approximately half of all states currently conduct some form of post-election audit
  • Audits must be transparent, allow public/partisan observation, and meet certification deadlines

3. Enhanced Testing & Certification

  • VVSG 2.0 establishes baseline cybersecurity, accessibility, and usability requirements
  • Mandatory penetration testing of federally certified systems
  • Proposed SECURE IT Act would codify testing requirements and create vulnerability disclosure program
  • Allow vetted researchers to test election systems in controlled environments

4. Federal Coordination & Sustained Funding

  • Strengthen CISA's authority as interagency coordinator for election infrastructure
  • Position EAC as clearinghouse for non-infrastructure best practices
  • Consolidate all election technology testing and certification within CISA
  • Establish predictable, sustained funding rather than sporadic emergency appropriations
  • Estimated need: $1.8 billion over next decade

5. Physical Security & Chain of Custody

  • Bipartisan teams (minimum two people) for all equipment access
  • Tamper-evident seals with documented serial numbers
  • Video surveillance of storage areas
  • Detailed chain-of-custody logging throughout equipment lifecycle
  • Secure storage in locked facilities with restricted access
  • Rigorous pre-election logic and accuracy testing

6. Minimize Network Connectivity

  • Ban cellular modems in voting equipment (EAC latest guidelines)
  • Prefer air-gapped systems isolated from internet connectivity
  • Remove or disable unnecessary wireless features
  • Use secure, verified transmission methods for unofficial results

7. Lifecycle Management & Replacement Planning

  • Plan for 10-year replacement cycles rather than waiting for equipment failure
  • Establish dedicated revenue sources or sinking funds for regular equipment replacement
  • Vendor contracts must include maintenance support and parts availability guarantees
  • State-level funding to ensure rural and small counties aren't left behind

8. Transparency & Public Communication

  • Public pre-election testing with observer access
  • Clear communication about security measures and incident response plans
  • Accessible audit processes with public results reporting
  • Proactive debunking of disinformation
  • Technical excellence must be accompanied by public transparency to build voter confidence
